PerAgent focuses on the critical systems-level challenges of transitioning from narrow, single-task edge AI to general-purpose, autonomous AI agents operating in physical environments. Currently, foundation models are trapped in the cloud due to massive computational requirements. This workshop explores how large-scale models such as Large Language Models (LLMs), Vision-Language Models (VLMs), and Multimodal Language Models (MLMs) can be compressed, decentralized, and securely integrated with pervasive sensor networks.

A defining feature of pervasive agentic systems is their domain-agnostic architectures. The exact same edge-computing architecture, split learning pipeline, and VLM integration used to fuse IoT telemetry and satellite data for a real-time decision-support system in precision agriculture can be deployed in a clinical setting to fuse wearable sensor streams for continuous patient monitoring. PerAgent investigates the underlying system architecture that remains constant even as the environmental context changes.
